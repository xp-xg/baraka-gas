HOW TO DETECT A GAS LEAK

Three Ways to Identify a Leak:

1SMELL

LPG has a distinctive odor (like rotten eggs or sulfur) added for safety. If you smell this odor, there may be a leak.

2SOUND

Listen for hissing or whistling sounds near the cylinder, regulator, or connections.

3SIGHT

Look for bubbles in soapy water applied to connections, frost formation on the cylinder, or dead vegetation near outdoor cylinders.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS - IF YOU SMELL GAS
1TURN OFF THE GAS

Immediately turn the cylinder valve clockwise to close it. Do this even if you're not sure where the leak is coming from.

2VENTILATE THE AREA

Open all windows and doors to allow gas to disperse. Do NOT use fans or create any sparks.

3EVACUATE

Get everyone out of the building immediately. Do not stop to gather belongings.

4CALL FOR HELP

Once outside and at a safe distance, call Baraka Gas emergency hotline: +254 721 489 755

FIRE EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
If LPG catches fire: Do NOT attempt to extinguish the fire unless you can safely shut off the gas supply first.
1EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY

Get everyone out of the building and move to a safe distance (at least 100 meters).

2CALL FIRE DEPARTMENT

Dial 999 or 112 for emergency services.

3CALL BARAKA GAS

Contact our emergency hotline: +254 721 489 755

4COOL SURROUNDING CYLINDERS

If safe to do so, use water to cool nearby cylinders to prevent explosion. Do this from a safe distance.

FIRST AID FOR GAS EXPOSURE

Symptoms of Gas Inhalation:

  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Loss of consciousness

First Aid Steps:

1MOVE TO FRESH AIR

Immediately move the person to fresh air. Do not re-enter the contaminated area.

2CHECK BREATHING

If the person is not breathing, begin CPR if you are trained to do so.

3SEEK MEDICAL ATTENTION

Call an ambulance immediately. Even if symptoms seem mild, medical evaluation is necessary.

4KEEP WARM

Keep the person warm and calm while waiting for medical help.

PREVENTION TIPS
  • Regular Inspections: Check your cylinder, regulator, and hose monthly for damage
  • Proper Installation: Always use certified technicians for installation
  • Ventilation: Ensure adequate ventilation in areas where LPG is used
  • Storage: Store cylinders upright in well-ventilated areas away from heat sources
  • Replacement: Replace hoses every 2 years and regulators every 5 years
  • Education: Ensure all family members know how to turn off the gas supply
